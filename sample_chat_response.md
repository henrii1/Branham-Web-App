Bro Branham taught that the serpent's seed originated in the Garden of Eden when Satan entered the serpent—a beast-like creature resembling a man, upright and subtle—and committed adultery with Eve, impregnating her before she was with Adam. This produced Cain as the serpent's literal seed, distinct from Eve's promised seed through Abel and later righteous lines like Seth, Noah, Abraham, and ultimately Christ, fulfilling Genesis 3:15's enmity between the woman's seed and the serpent's seed. Evidence: [THE SERPENT’S SEED — 58-0928E: ¶157–¶163; ¶164–¶171]

The serpent's seed line produced intelligent, religious but unrighteous people—shrewd builders, scientists, and giants (like pre-flood Canaanites)—contrasting the humble, God-fearing seed of the woman, with ongoing enmity seen in modern educated skeptics, immorality, and denominationalism versus true faith. Cain's descendants went to Nod, intermarried, and perpetuated this hybrid, corrupt lineage destroyed in the flood except through Noah's righteous line. Evidence: [THE SERPENT’S SEED — 58-0928E: ¶179–¶183; ¶191–¶196; QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS — 59-0628E: ¶174–¶177; QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS — 64-0823M: ¶106–¶109]

He emphasized "beguiled" in Genesis 3 means "defiled" sexually, not eating fruit, explaining giants and the "missing link" science seeks, as the serpent was the only creature whose seed could mix with woman's; Eve bore twins from two fathers, Cain (serpent's seed via Satan) and Abel. This seed manifests today in worldly wisdom, adultery-influenced culture (e.g., movies, rock-and-roll), and opposition to God's Word. Evidence: [THE SERPENT’S SEED — 58-0928E: ¶157–¶163; QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS — 64-0830M: ¶312–¶318; CONDEMNATION BY REPRESENTATION — 60-1113: ¶96–¶100]

tell me how esau fits in?
Bro Branham taught Esau as an example of someone from the righteous seed line of the woman (through Seth, Noah, Shem, Abraham, and Isaac), yet rejected by God in election before birth, contrasting with Jacob who was chosen, to illustrate predestination and enmity between seeds rather than making Esau part of the serpent's seed from Cain. Esau is described as a good, moral man—a gentleman who cared for his father and never harmed anyone—but "of the devil" in the sense of not being elected, while Jacob from the same womb was "of God," paralleling Cain (serpent's seed, religious but rejected) and Abel. This fits the serpent seed doctrine by showing how even within the woman's seed line, God sovereignly separates the elect (like Jacob) from the non-elect (like Esau), maintaining enmity and fulfilling His purpose without placing Esau in Cain's lineage. Evidence: [THE SERPENT’S SEED — 58-0928E: ¶260–¶261; ISRAEL AND THE CHURCH 1 — 53-0325: ¶131–¶133]
